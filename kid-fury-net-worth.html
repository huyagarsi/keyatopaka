    Kid Fury Full Overview and Wiki

    Early Life and Rise to Fame

    Born Gregory A. Smith on November 24, 1987, in Miami, Florida, Kid Fury grew up in a Jamaican American household.

    His early life in Miami laid the foundation for his later success. From a young age, Fury was drawn to comedy, finding inspiration in shows like In Living Color, Martin, and Moesha. This early exposure to the world of entertainment sparked his passion for humor and storytelling.

    The Digital Platform Launch

    In 2010, he launched his YouTube channel, marking the beginning of his journey as a digital content creator. His channel, known for the Furious Thoughts vlog series, quickly gained traction, amassing over 10 million views by 2013.

    This success on YouTube was not just about numbers; it was about establishing Fury as a unique voice in the digital space. His unfiltered and humorous take on pop culture resonated with a wide audience, earning him the title of Black Twitter’s Kingmaker.

    Podcasting Success with The Read

    His venture into podcasting proved to be a pivotal move in his career. Alongside Crissle West, he co-hosts The Read, a podcast that averages 400,000 listeners per episode.
